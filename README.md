# 🚇 Delhi Metro Data Analysis Project
Analyzed metro travel data using Python (Pandas) and MySQL to identify passenger trends, high-demand routes, and revenue patterns. Cleaned raw JSON data, structured it into a database, and performed SQL queries to extract insights on station usage, ticket types, and travel behavior.

 ## 📌 Objective
The goal of this project is to analyze metro travel data to understand passenger behavior, identify high-demand routes, and study revenue patterns.

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used

- MySQL
- VS Code

1. Install dependencies:

   python src/data_cleaning.py
3. Execute SQL queries in MySQL Workbench

## 📂 Dataset Information
- Source: Delhi Metro travel dataset (JSON format)
- Records: 10,000 trips
- Features:
  - TripID
  - Date
  - From_Station
  - To_Station
  - Distance_km
  - Fare
  - Passengers
  - Ticket_Type
  - Remarks

 ## 🔄 Project Workflow

1. **Data Cleaning (Python)**
   - Loaded raw JSON dataset
   - Handled missing values
   - Standardized columns
   - Converted data into CSV format

2. **Database Creation (MySQL)**
   - Created database `metro_db`
   - Designed table `metro_trips`
   - Imported cleaned dataset

3. **SQL Analysis**
   - Performed 20 analytical queries
   - Used GROUP BY, SUM, AVG, ORDER BY
   - Analyzed routes, stations, revenue, and passengers

 ## 📊 Insights and Findings

 🚇 Route-Level Insights
- The route Mandi House → Laxmi Nagar recorded the highest passenger count (37 passengers in a single trip), indicating it is one of the busiest short-distance routes.
- Routes originating from Rajiv Chowk dominate in revenue generation, with Rajiv Chowk → Chandni Chowk (~₹ 9493) being the highest earning route.
- Multiple top revenue routes originate from Rajiv Chowk, confirming it as a central and high-demand transit hub.
- Frequently occurring station pairs such as Rajiv Chowk → Chandni Chowk (88 trips) indicate strong and repetitive commuting patterns.

🚉 Station-Level Insights
- Rajiv Chowk has the highest number of departures (1675 trips), making it the busiest starting station in the network.
- Stations like Kirti Nagar (8130 passengers) and Kashmere Gate (8127 passengers) receive the highest passenger inflow, indicating strong destination demand.
- A small group of stations (Rajiv Chowk, New Delhi, Mandi House) handle the majority of metro activity, showing centralized usage.
- High departure and arrival counts highlight major interchange stations in the metro system.

💰 Revenue Insights
- The total revenue generated by the metro system is approximately (₹ 103,381), showing strong financial performance.
- The average fare per trip is around (₹ 104.32), indicating affordable pricing for commuters.
- Revenue is heavily concentrated on routes connected to Rajiv Chowk, reinforcing its importance in the network.
- Certain routes generate high revenue despite moderate distances, indicating high passenger turnover.

📏 Distance & Efficiency Insights
- The longest route observed is New Delhi → Mandi House (45.69 km), followed by other long-distance routes exceeding 40 km.
- Routes such as Dilshad Garden → Dilshad Garden show the highest revenue per km (~255), indicating highly efficient or dense passenger flow.
- Variations in revenue per km suggest differences in route efficiency and passenger distribution.

👥 Passenger Behavior Insights
- The average number of passengers per trip is approximately 20, indicating steady utilization across the network.
- Peak trips reach up to 37 passengers, highlighting congestion during busy routes or time periods.
- Passenger distribution is uneven, with certain routes experiencing significantly higher demand.

🎫 Ticket Usage Insights
- Tourist Card is the most used ticket type (4802 uses, 96,239 passengers), indicating a high proportion of occasional or flexible travelers.
- Smart Card and Single tickets are used less frequently compared to Tourist Cards.
- This suggests that passengers prefer bundled or multi-use ticket options over one-time travel tickets.

⏱️ Travel Pattern Insights
- Travel distribution across conditions is relatively balanced:
Peak: 1600 trips
Off-peak: 1644 trips
Festival: 1661 trips
Weekend: 1637 trips
Maintenance: 1634 trips
This indicates that metro usage is consistent across different travel conditions, not just peak hours.

💸 Revenue by Travel Condition
- The highest revenue is generated during peak periods (~₹ 179,824), followed closely by off-peak and weekend travel.
- Even non-peak conditions generate substantial revenue, showing stable demand throughout different conditions.

📅 Time-Based Insights
- Passenger traffic is highest in Month 1 (17,334 passengers) and lowest around Month 11 (~15,561 passengers), showing moderate seasonal variation.
- Revenue follows a similar pattern, with Month 1 (~₹ 92,239) being the highest.
- Monthly fluctuations indicate possible seasonal trends but overall stable metro usage.

🔄 Operational Insights
- The metro system is highly centralized, with Rajiv Chowk acting as the core hub for both passenger traffic and revenue.
- A limited number of routes and stations handle a large portion of total activity, which may lead to congestion.
- Consistent passenger flow across different conditions indicates reliability of metro services.
- Identifying high-demand routes can help optimize train frequency and reduce overcrowding.
